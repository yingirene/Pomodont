POMODON'T

April 2015 - Java - Repo

Right-click menu dialog.

Inspiration

I was really into finding different ways to manage time and become more productive, and one method that really interested me was the Pomodoro technique.

Why make this app

At the time, I felt like I had learned enough about how to program to apply my knowledge into a full-blown project. I put together features of some of my favorite timers and features that I couldn't find in any existing solutions and took the chance to develop my own Pomodoro timer.

What it does

A timer that tracks elapsed time as well as time spent working on a task.
  • Take breaks of alternating lengths.
  • See how productive you are by comparing elapsed vs. working time.
  • Graphics change depending on what stage of the Pomodoro technique you're at.
    • (e.g. On Break, Working, Distracted, Idle)

How I built it

I was pretty new to coding, so everything was kind of just thrown together without much structure planned beforehand.

Challenges I ran into

  • I found myself needing to make more and more classes as I made progress and the code was getting very messy.
